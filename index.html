NPL Market Context — Thailand AMC Industry
Thai NPL market NPL/total loans ratio: ~86% of GDP in household debt (2025). NPL in banking system remained elevated with banks cautious on new lending.
BOT-licensed NPL portfolio sales slowed in 2025. NPL inflow to AMC market declined as banks prefer internal restructuring over market disposal.
NPA (foreclosed property) market soft: Bangkok residential appraisal value ≥ 50% of BAM's NPA. Disposal remains challenging amid weak RE market.
BOT JV-AMC policy enabling AMC-bank joint ventures (like JK AMC) may expand NPL supply to selective players.
Total NPL face value managed by BAM alone: THB 491,112 MB (vs. THB 109,833 MB net book = significant ECL buffer on balance sheet).

B
BAM — Bangkok Commercial Asset Management
Thailand's Largest DAMC (Distressed Asset Management Co.) · A−/Negative

Strategic Focus

"Recycling Machine" strategy — continuous cycle of buying NPLs/NPAs from banks, collecting/restructuring, and disposing NPA via market channels to redeploy capital.
BAMX Digital Transformation — deploying AI/ML in NPL scoring (Agentplus), digital NPA marketplace (BAM SELECT, BAM มหาชน), and legal collection process automation.
RPL conversion — converting non-performing to re-performing loans (RPL) to generate recurring yield rather than full write-off/sale.
JV-AMC strategy — partnering with banks (JV AMC) for large-scale portfolio transfers. FY2026 JV AMC investment target = THB 600,000 MB AUM.
FY2026 targets: Cash collection THB 17,900 MB; Net profit THB 2,000 MB.

Key Challenges

Credit rating on Negative Outlook — risk of further downgrade if collections remain below forecast; investor confidence at stake.
NPA disposal increasingly difficult — 50% of NPA portfolio is residential RE in Bangkok, where market is soft and buyer demand limited.
High leverage at D/E 2.03× limits new acquisition capacity; debt/equity ceiling creates funding constraints in a rising rate environment.
Q4 2025 net profit fell 77.6% YoY to THB 117 MB — a single-quarter sign of margin pressure from higher ECL and slower NPA monetization.
ECL burden at THB 4,639 MB (34.6% of revenue) — largest absolute ECL charge in sector; reflects asset pricing risk on the enormous inherited portfolio.
J
JMT — JMT Network Services PCL
Thailand's Leading Unsecured NPL Investor & Manager · BBB/Stable (Downgraded from BBB+)

Strategic Focus

Revenue & ECL Management — managing ECL to appropriate levels via settlement discount (partial forgiveness), helping borrowers restructure in line with current income, legal collection acceleration.
JK AMC JV with KBANK — strategic JV managing corporate NPLs. JK AMC has acquired over THB 100B in NPLs from KBANK since inception 2022, providing JMT a steady pipeline beyond retail unsecured.
AI-based collection — OCR technology and AI-based Quality Monitoring System to improve agent efficiency and compliance. JMT's tech moat protects margins as volume slows.
Capital structure management — FY2025: repaid THB 6,376 MB in bonds/loans, issued new THB 4,162 MB. FY2026 obligations = THB 2,413.6 MB — manageable given operating cash flow.

Key Challenges

Cash collection declining — FY2025 own collection = THB 5,090 MB (down 9% YoY). TRIS expects recovery to remain weak in 2026 given fragile household finances.
Net profit down 36% YoY — driven by lower revenue (−12.3%) and higher ECL (THB 891 MB). TRIS downgraded to BBB, reflecting the financial deterioration.
Deteriorating borrower credit quality — household DSR at record highs; unsecured NPL borrowers facing reduced serviceability, pushing collection toward more legal action (slow & costly).
JMART Group dependency risk — JMT accounts for 68% of JMART Group assets; any adverse JMART-level event cascades directly to JMT rating and funding access.
C
CHAYO — Chayo Group PCL
Dual-Engine NPL Investor + Debt Tracking Service Provider · Not Rated

Strategic Focus

Unsecured NPL portfolio owner — focuses on buying unsecured NPLs from banks at steep discounts. Collection via own collection teams and legal channels (NPA conversion to THB 36.28 MB in FY2025).
Debt tracking outsource services — revenue from tracking/enforcement services for third-party banks (outsourced collection). Growing as banks reduce own teams; THB 28.42 MB in FY2025 revenue.
5-year ECL reserve policy — provisioning policy requires full ECL reserve for unsecured NPL held >5 years, creating a large but transparent balance sheet ECL stock (THB 2,052 MB = 136% of NPL net value).
Leverage expansion for growth — raised IBD from banks to THB 4,299 MB (IBD/E = 1.05×). IPO listing (sub-company) to diversify funding and attract capital for NPL acquisition pipeline.

Key Challenges

Net profit down ~44% YoY to THB 206 MB — ECL charge jumped to THB 338 MB (from 176 MB prior year) as portfolio ages and the 5-year provision trigger fires on older unsecured accounts.
High cost of fund at 6.80% — limits purchase price competitiveness vs. BAM and JMT when bidding for quality portfolios. Needs high yield from portfolio to maintain spread.
Scale disadvantage — at THB 8.8B total assets, CHAYO cannot access the large institutional tranches that BAM or JMT secure, limiting portfolio diversification.
Concentration in unsecured NPL — fully exposed to household financial stress with no NPA/collateral buffer; economic deterioration amplifies ECL and collection risk disproportionately.
Ch
Chase — Chase Asia PCL
Outsourced Collection Agency + Small NPL Investor · Not Rated

Strategic Focus

Outsourced debt tracking & enforcement — primary business is fee-based collection services for banks (success fee model). Revenue = THB 194.5 MB (26% of total). Growing client base with legal channel capabilities.
NPL portfolio wind-down strategy — no new NPL acquisitions since 2022. Existing THB 40,240 MB face value NPL portfolio being worked down through collection (THB 457.9 MB collected in FY2025).
Legal collection focus — "Success Rate" at 4.0% on tracked NPL (THB 666.1 MB collected out of THB 17,634 MB tracked). Commission rate at 26.8%.
Group collection acceleration — parent group strategy to exit NPL investment business and focus purely on fee-income collection services where capital is not tied up.

Key Challenges

Near-zero profitability — FY2025 net profit only THB 2.2 MB (down 97.4% YoY). ECL charge of THB 317.3 MB wiped out operating profit. Company is essentially at break-even.
Highest ECL/Revenue ratio at 43% — ECL charge disproportionate to revenue base, reflecting conservative provisioning on the aged NPL portfolio that was acquired at high prices pre-2022.
NPL portfolio in structural decline — net NPL dropped to THB 2,852 MB (from THB 3,084 MB), no replenishment. Business model shift from principal investor to pure service provider reduces earnings power.
High cost of fund 7.14% — on a declining and non-replenished portfolio, cost of carry is unsustainable. Refinancing risk in 2026 with THB 180.2 MB coming due.
K
KCC HLD — Nai Klub Capital Holdings PCL
Niche Corporate NPL Specialist (KCC AMC + KCC AR) · Not Rated · Strongest Net Profit Growth +199%

Strategic Focus

Selective Portfolio Expansion ("Cherry Picking") — focuses exclusively on corporate secured NPLs with clear collateral and legal resolution path. Avoids retail unsecured. Strategy: quality over volume.
"Harvesting Phase" for legacy portfolios — transitioning older portfolios to cash realization (closing files, enforcing collateral) to accelerate IRR. Portfolio IRR-focused rather than yield-per-year.
Accelerated Monetization strategy — fast-track NPA disposal and NPL enforcement to shorten holding periods and improve asset turnover (Asset Turn improvement).
Cost & Capital Efficiency — target to expand Net Profit Margin and Free Cash Flow. Operating Leverage model benefits from growing revenue on near-fixed cost base. FY2025 NPM = 51.0%.
Diversification & Risk Management — expand into corporate/housing/asset-backed NPL types. Manage ECL conservatively and maintain adequate liquidity buffer.

Key Challenges

Smallest scale in sector — THB 2,746 MB total assets limits ability to win large NPL auctions or outbid BAM/JMT. Dependent on niche corporate secured market that has limited supply.
High cost of fund at 6.95% — despite achieving 51% NPM, the high funding cost limits the discount at which KCC can purchase assets and still achieve satisfactory IRR.
NPA monetization timing — NPA collection was impacted by sluggish real estate market. NPA is expected to remain an optional income stream in 2026 with market timing risk.
High FY2025 growth may not repeat — 199% profit growth was partly driven by unrealized gains (fair value adjustments) and large transactions. Recurring earnings visibility is limited without consistent large-ticket acquisitions.
